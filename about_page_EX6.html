Jennifer Huseman

Jennifer Huseman is a Clinical Education Coordinator/Asst Professor at Metropolitan State University of Denver. Prior to joining Metropolitan State University of Denver, she was Instructor/Assistant Athletic Trainer at University of Central Missouri. Jennifer earned her Doctor of Education (EdD) from University of Missouri-Columbia and is currently pursuing a master’s degree in public health from Grand Canyon University. Jennifer has been married to Benjamin Huseman for over fifteen years and has a daughter, Tristin. Being pet lovers, there have been numerous dogs over time with the families’ current companions being an English Bulldog named Blue and American Mastiff named Tosca.

Jennifer currently volunteers her time as the Vice President Board of Directors for Community Uplift Partnership with a focus on strategic planning and grant writing. She is also a Peer Reviewer for the Commission on Accreditation of Athletic Training Education (CAATE), as well as the Secretary of the Colorado Athletic Trainers Association. Jennifer enjoys several hobbies, especially those involving the outdoors. An avid hiker, mountain biker and adventurer, the family frequently packs up and takes their camper to various destinations across the country. Teaching her daughter Tristin to ride horses and then going out together on trail rides. Finally riding motorcycles both around town and on the track with her husband Benjamin. Being an athletic trainer by trade means Jennifer has a strong love and appreciation for sports and physical activities. A die-hard Kansas City Chiefs and Royals fan, she loves to watch the teams play and catch games in person when the schedule permits. Jennifer believes in always learning new things and never getting too comfortable, she looks forward to completing her Masters in Public Health and moving onto the next challenge.
